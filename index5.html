Latitude vs. Wind Speed (Miles Per Hour)

Further analysis is required to determine statistical significance of the slightly positive trend that emerges as latitude increases with regards to wind speed. NASA identifies global wind patterns by latitude accordingly; "Trade Winds" at 30 degrees North and South of the equator, "Prevailing Westerlies" between 40 to 60 degrees in both hemispheres and "Polar Easterlies" at 50 to 60 degrees in both hemisperes.
